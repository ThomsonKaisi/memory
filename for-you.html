Dear Memory, my Real Mama,

I've been thinking about yesterday, and about us. I'm not writing this to ask for anything. I just wanted you to know what's in my heart, because you matter too much to me for it to go unsaid.

I'm sorry for the things I said to you. Those words were unkind, and they never should have come out of my mouth, not toward the woman I love. My reaction was wrong, and it should never have become physical like that. There is no excuse for it, and I don't offer one. You didn't deserve any of it, and no fight, no frustration, no bad day will ever make it okay. I hurt you, and I own that completely.

Sitting with Tan wasn't easy, but I'm grateful we did it, because it reminded me that you and I are worth every hard conversation. I'd rather do difficult things with you than easy things without you. You are still, and always, the best part of my days.

So take all the time you need to reflect. I mean that. There's no clock on this, and I'm not going anywhere. My heart chose you a long time ago, Memory, and it hasn't changed its mind. When you're ready to talk, tomorrow, next week, whenever, I'll be here, ready to listen properly this time, and to love you better than I did yesterday.

And remember, Memory: you are my calm amidst chaos. You always have been.

With all my heart, always,
Thomson, your Oreo
